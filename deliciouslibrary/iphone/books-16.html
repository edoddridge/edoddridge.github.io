Remembering BabylonDavid Malouf  

A searing and magnificent picture of Australia at the moment of its foundation, with early settlers staking out their small patch of land and terrified by the harsh and alien continent. Focussing on the hostility between the early British inhabitants and the native Aboriginals. REMEMBERING BABYLON tells the tragic and compelling story of a boy who finds himself caught between the two worlds. Shot through with humour, and written with the poetic intensity that characterised Malouf's AN IMAGINARY LIFE this is the novel of epic scope yet it is simple, compassionate and universal: a classic.

The Children of the New ForestFrederick Marryat, Dennis Butts  

In The Children of the New Forest, Marryat describes the trials and triumphs of the four Beverley children, orphaned during the English Civil War and forced to take refuge with a poor woodsman in the New Forest. This is the first annotated edition of a great children's classic, which has retained its popularity since 1847.
